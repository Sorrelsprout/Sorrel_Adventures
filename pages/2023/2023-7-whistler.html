Border crossing

The border crossing was pretty quick! It felt a bit nervous when talking with the border agent but all we did was pass them our passports, they asked some questions, and then we passed right through.

We went to Tim Horton’s right after crossing, and Sam got a coffee and a donut.

Lavender Land

Smelled so good, and we took many photos. The best smelling kind in my opinion is the Melissa Lilac (Lavendula angustifolia)

For lunch we went to see what kind of food the temple had, and decided on their straw mushrooms fried with veggies and bamboo shoots. It was really tasty!

Stawamus Chief

Ssuper intense hike with gloriously tall trees and waterfalls, got to a rocky patch where we had to climb using a pre-installed rope at a 45-degree-ish incline up a rock face.

2nd peak. Sam wanted to go to the 3rd peak but I didn’t want to because I was so tired and we ran out of water.

Getting back down we didn’t go the exact same way we came from, and there were giant orangey-sequoias.

2nd AirBnB in Whistler

Walked around, looked like a ski camp similar to Truckee at Tahoe. Had ponds that were light blue-colored, due to the minerals in the water. Saw a moth in the grass with a broken wing and it was fluttering really fast. Felt bad for it but there was nothing we could do.

There was a concert going on in the main stage area right where our AirBnB balcony looks out at. The sound was very loud and we could hear it very much in the room.

Thursday — Jul 6th, 2023

Whistler

Mountain biking for the first time. We took a gondola up and it was difficult to get in. I felt embarrassed because I couldn’t get my bike in the lift and sit, and the attendant had to help me slide in and placed the bike. The bike pinned my helmet and I couldn’t move my face.

Felt like I was getting a concussion riding down.

Friday — Jul 7th, 2023

Blackcomb Mountain

Hiking and took the gondola. The scenery was beautiful and there were so many wildflowers. Particularly memorable was an orange mountain dandelion (Agoseris sp.), along with wispy truffula-tree-looking plants. We were so high up that we could see snow too.

Greenish-blue lake at top.

Mosquito bite was intensely itchy, and it was making my leg swell up. I had a pus bubble form and tried my best not to touch it. I had to limp to walk back and the skin around the bite was stretching so much that my leg was sore. I kept putting Tiger Balm on it.

First Nations Heritage Center

We wanted to check out the center, but they had a private event going on. As we exited, there was a man outside carving a house post. Samarth took a lot of interest and started talking to him, which was how we met Xwalacktun

Hospital

We went to the hospital right around where our AirBnB is, and the front desk staff actually took a brief peek at my leg. They suggested I head back to the states because it would be $12K if I even want to be seen.

AirBnB

There was another concert going on. I wasn’t too interested and just wanted my bite to heal.

Saturday — Jul 8th, 2023

Washington

My leg was so bad that the pus bubble swelled to the diameter of a quarter. We quickly packed and Sam drove us back to Washington where I could be seen by a Zoomcare doctor. He said it wasn’t sepsis so that’s good, and that it should go down, and that I should take Zyrtec/allergy meds to basically wait it out.

Sam drove us back 8 hours, since it was best I didn’t drive.
