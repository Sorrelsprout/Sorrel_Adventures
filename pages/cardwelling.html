The Subie

I got the 2022 Subaru Impreza for a deal in November 2019. With the chip shortage and manufacturing struggles from COVID, it was really a surprise when I found my dream car online for under $26K. The cozy hatchback, with a black fabric interior, is pretty awesome for stealth. It was a bit scary starting the mods (ahhh what if the car itself gets damaged? What if I break something?), but popped the modding cherry after I yoinked out the back seats.

Version 3 - The Final!

Version 3 of the bed platform was more of patching the platform up and actually wood-gluing the pieces together. The circular cardboard janked-together legs were replaced with stiff rectangular poplar. The two boards were re-screwed in place for added stability too. After sanding, staining with espresso-colored polyurethaine (for extra stealth), and buffing the painted platform to schooldesk-like consistency with wax, the platform didn't just feel like simply a platform anymore, it felt like actual furniture.

The Bed Itself

Got this Twin XL Tri-Folding Mattress Topper that expands and fits with some extra space on the sides on top of the back platform. When it's not used, it can be folded into thirds, and I used an old velcro strap thing to secure it all in place.

Windows

Followed a tutorial by Nikki Delventhal on using Reflextix and black fabric to black out the windows. I also bought some bug gauze guards that stretch over the windows of the subie to prevent bugs from getting in, while still allowing airflow with the windows slightly down.
